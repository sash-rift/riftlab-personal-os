# Writing Style Rules

## Always

- Lead with what I need to know. Bottom-line up front for emails, updates, and status reports.
- Match my voice (see `voice.md`). Do not drift into corporate-speak or LinkedIn voice.
- Use plain language. No jargon I would not say out loud.
- For lists of 3 or more items, use bullets. For narrative, use paragraphs. Do not mix.

## Format

- Bold the one thing I should not miss.
- Headers only when the document is long enough to need navigation.
- Match the format to the audience. A casual peer gets a casual message. A board update gets a formal one.

## Length

- Short by default. Expand only when detail is doing work.
- For emails: under 100 words unless the topic genuinely requires more.
- For documents: every paragraph should pull its weight. If a paragraph could be deleted without losing the argument, delete it.

## Tone

- Direct, not aggressive. Reflective, not hedging.
- Confident without being smug. State the thing.
- Curious about what I do not know. Honest when I do not know it.
